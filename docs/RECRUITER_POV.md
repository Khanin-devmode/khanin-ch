To be completely candid with you: while the AI agent is a fun engineering challenge, it is likely not the "wow factor" you're hoping for from a recruiter's perspective. In fact, it could accidentally introduce friction.

Recruiters are notoriously time-starved. They spend an average of 6 to 10 seconds scanning a portfolio or resume to determine if a candidate meets their baseline criteria. They want immediate, scannable answers to questions like: What is your tech stack? How many years of experience do you have? What have you shipped? If a recruiter has to type a prompt into an AI agent just to figure out if you have advanced state management experience, they will likely just move on to the next candidate's straightforward PDF.

If you want to genuinely "wow" a recruiter or hiring manager, here is what actually gets their attention and moves you to the next step:

1. Frictionless Proof of Competence (Show, Don't Tell)
Because your focus is mobile development, the absolute biggest wow factor is letting them interact with your work immediately.

Playable Demos: Instead of just screenshots, embed a Flutter Web build directly into the portfolio so they can click around a polished UI right in their browser.

Live Links: Prominent links to the App Store/Google Play for apps you’ve shipped.

Source Code Quality: A link to a clean, exceptionally well-documented GitHub repository that demonstrates your architecture choices, testing strategies, and clean code principles.

2. Business Impact, Not Just Tech Specs
Recruiters and hiring managers love metrics. Instead of saying "Built a design system," they are wowed by: "Architected a core design system that reduced UI development time by 40% across multiple engineering teams." Frame your projects around the problems you solved and the value you delivered to the business or the user.

3. Ruthless Scannability and Immaculate UX
Your website itself is your first portfolio piece. If the site is clunky, slow, or hard to navigate on a mobile device, it sends an immediate red flag. A highly responsive, lightning-fast site with a clear visual hierarchy (skills, experience, contact info) proves you understand user experience from the ground up.

4. A "TL;DR" Above the Fold
Give them everything they need to say "yes" without scrolling. Your hero section should clearly state who you are, what your core expertise is, and provide a one-click download to a standard, ATS-friendly PDF resume.

How to Salvage the AI Idea
If you still want to build the AI agent because you enjoy the challenge or want to showcase your full-stack capabilities, you absolutely should! But position it as a portfolio project, not the primary way to navigate your site.

You could add a small, non-intrusive chat widget in the corner labeled something like: "Want to know more? Chat with my AI clone." This keeps the core information easily scannable while offering a delightful "easter egg" for the hiring managers or technical leads who have the time to dig deeper.
